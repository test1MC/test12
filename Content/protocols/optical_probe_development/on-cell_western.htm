On-Cell Western

The On-Cell Western assay is a Receptor-based binding assay, and may include dose response and competitive binding evaluations where relevant.

For the full procedure, see the On-Cell Western Protocol.

  1. Prepare cell culture in 96- or 384-well microtiter plate.

  2. Prepare serial dilutions of the labeled targeting agent to identify the effective concentration for further in vitro assay analyses.

  3. Apply chemical inhibitors, stimulants, or drugs to induce a detectable response.

    Be sure to include positive and negative controls to assess non-specific binding and/or background effects.

  4. Prepare labeled targeting agent for incubation and detection of a cellular or receptor-based response, with or without unlabeled blocking agents or competitive agents. This process is conducted on live cells so it is important to maintain cell health during the procedures.

  5. Rinse and fix cells. No permeabilization is necessary.

  6. Normalization of cell number allows ratiometric analysis of signals. Be sure to use a complementary dye for normalization to be captured at a different wavelength from your labeled targeting agent. eg, IRDye 800CW-EGF (800nm) and Draq5 (700nm).

    • Cell permeable dyes that stain DNA (Draq5)

    • Cell membrane stains (NHS esters).

  7. Rinse.

  8. Scan the plate on an Odyssey CLx and use Image Studio Software for analysis (see Analysis below).
