In-Cell Western

Validate primary antibody for use with ICW:

  • Antibody Validation for In-Cell Western Assay (Doc #: 982-12580)
  • Z-Factor Analysis (Doc #: 982-10620)
  • Z-Factor Application Note (Doc #:979-10610)

 

Prepare cell culture in 96- or 384-well microtiter plate.

  • Plate Selection and Instrument Settings
  • Seeding Cells (Doc #: 982-10597)
  • Frequently Asked Questions for Cell Suspension in In-Cell Western Assays (Doc #: 988-11461)
  • Printable Microplate Setup Guide

 

Apply your treatment, (e.g., chemical inhibitors, stimulants, or drugs) to induce a response.

Be sure to include controls.

  • Positive control: Used to confirm that your target is expressed on the relevant cells and tissues.
  • Negative control: Used to confirm that your target is not found in tissues or cells where your target is supposed to be absent.

Use several positive and negative controls.

 

Rinse, fix and permeabilize cells as described in the In-Cell Western Fixation and Permeabilization Protocol.

 

Incubate with primary antibodies.

  • Target specific
  • For cell normalization
    • pan antibody (tubulin, actin, etc)
    • total protein stain
    • DNA stain (CellTag 700, Draq 5, TO-PRO-3 or other)
    • Membrane stain (NHS ester)

 

Rinse and incubate with IRDye-labeled secondary antibodies.

Be aware of host species when selecting primary and secondary antibodies.

 

Rinse.

 

Scan the plate on an Odyssey CLx and use use Image Studio Software for analysis (SEE ANALYSIS BELOW).

To learn more about the In-Cell Western assay, see A Cell Based Immunocytochemical Assay for Monitoring Kinase Signaling Pathways and Drug Efficacy.
