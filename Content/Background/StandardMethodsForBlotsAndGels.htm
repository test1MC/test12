Average or Median (Local Methods)

  • If only a few shapes need to be quantified, adding the shapes individually and using local background methods can work well.

  • Choose instead of to reduce the impact of outlier pixel intensities in the background.

  • If shapes are in a lane containing many nonspecific bands and the background is uniform around the shapes, local methods may produce better results than the method.

  • The or background method computes the average or median intensity of pixels in a border around a shape and subtracts that background from the shape. The width and position of the border can be adjusted.
  • Background must be fairly uniform throughout an image.
  • Use when is not available and shapes will make it difficult to use local methods without having background border overlap shapes.
  • Use when the background is uniform throughout the image and bands are so close the background border from local methods might overlap a band.
  • Background is often not uniform enough for to accurately represent background for every shape in an image.
  • The background method finds the average pixel intensity within a shape or shapes placed by the user to calculate background. When multiple shapes are used, the average pixel intensity in each will be averaged together to find the background.
