Power Cords

The is equipped with a 3-wire grounding-type plug. This plug will only fit into a grounding-type outlet. This is a necessary safety feature. If you are unable to insert the plug into the outlet, you will need to replace the outlet. Do not defeat the purpose of the grounding-type plug.

Do not locate the where the power cord will be walked on or exposed to water or chemical spills.

The draws at most 4 amperes at 120V. If an extension cord is used, make sure the total of the ampere ratings on the instruments plugged into the extension cord does not exceed the extension cord ampere rating. Also make sure the total amperage of instruments plugged into the wall outlet does not exceed the amperage capacity for the outlet (usually 15 or 20 amperes in the United States).

To disconnect power before servicing, first disconnect the instrument from the software, briefly press the power button to power down the instrument, and then remove the power cord from the power receptacle on the back panel (Figure 1‑1). See Disconnecting the Power on page 1 for more information.
