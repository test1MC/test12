BioStack Z-Axis Training

The final procedure is to adjust the vertical (Z-axis) travel of the BioStack plate gripper. The goal is for microplates to be placed on the plate carrier from the optimal height. If the value of the vertical travel distance on the Z-axis is too small, the plate will drop onto the plate carrier rather than being placed on it. If the value of the vertical travel distance is too large, the plate will hit the plate carrier and the plate gripper arms will continue to travel downward, which could result in the bottom of the gripper assembly impacting the microplate as it rests on the plate carrier.

  1. Start on your computer. Open the application menu, hover over Instrument, Management, and click Administration.
  2. Click BioTek BioStack in the System Administration window.
  4. Click Z-Axis Training in the BioTek® BioStack™ window.
  6. Click Start in the Z-axis training window. This moves the BioStack plate gripper to its highest vertical position and opens the plate carrier.
  8. Use the supplied calibration ruler to measure, in millimeters, from the bottom tip of the plate gripper to the top surface of the plate carrier.
  10. Enter the measured Z-axis distance plus three millimeters (integers only) in the Move Amount (mm) field, or use the arrows to set a value. Three millimeters are added because the gripper arms should travel just below the upper surface of the plate carrier.
  11. Click Move Down. The bottom edge of the gripper should travel slightly below the top surface of the plate carrier and the bend in the gripper arm that a plate normally rests on should be slightly below the top surface of the plate carrier as shown below.
  13. If the gripper stops above the Odyssey Sa plate carrier, put one millimeter in the Move Amount and click Move Down again. Conversely, if the gripper arms travel downward more than half way through the thickness of the plate carrier, put one millimeter in the Move Amount and click Move Up. Repeat the corrections until gripper arm stops just in the correct position.
  14. Note: Always enter a “move amount” that is relative to the current position. For example, if your initial “move amount” is 60 and you need to add 1 millimeter, you should enter 1 rather than 61.
  15. Click Done in the Z-axis Training window and close all of the System Administration windows.

The BioTek® BioStack™ Plate Stacker is now ready for operation. Consult the BioStack Operator’s Manual supplied by BioTek for instructions on loading the input and output stacks. Note, however, that the Image Studio software expects microplates on the plate carrier to be in a certain orientation. If the input stacks are loaded randomly, some images will be right side up and others upside down.

Note: The membrane carrier is not compatible with the BioStack Plate Stacker.
