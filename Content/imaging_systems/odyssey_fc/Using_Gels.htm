Using Gels

DNA gels that are stained with ethidium bromide or other comparable stains (such as SYBR® Safe) can be imaged in the 600 nm channel. A detailed technical note on imaging DNA gels can be found in the Odyssey Fc Imager Application Protocols manual. Coomassie-stained gels can also be imaged since Coomassie Blue dye can be seen clearly in the 700 nm channel, and faintly in the 800 nm channel. To image a gel, observe the following guidelines:

  • Thoroughly rinse the gel with destaining solution or water to remove dye particulates.
  • When placing the gel in the imaging tray, place the top toward the back of the instrument.
