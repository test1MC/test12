Moving the Instrument

LI-COR recommends that you do not move your instrument. Please contact your local for LI-COR recommended service options. In extreme situations, you may use the procedure below to move your instrument.

These instructions are provided as a courtesy for our customers. cannot be held responsible for any damage incurred while moving, transporting, or shipping an , even if an existing warranty and/or service contract applies.

To move your instrument:

  1. Ensure the new location meets all requirements specified in Placement in the Laboratory on page 1Placement in the Laboratory on page 1Placement in the Laboratory on page 1Placement in the Laboratory on page 1.
  2. Turn off the by pressing on the front panel.
  3. Exit .
  4. Disconnect the power cord. See Power Cords and Disconnecting Power on page 1 for more information about disconnecting power.
  5. Disconnect the power cord. See Power Cords and Disconnecting Power on page 1 for more information about disconnecting power.
  6. Be cautious and lift with two people (the instrument weighs about ).
  7. Each person should place one hand in the lower hand hold and the other hand at the top of the instrument to stabilize it and keep it as near vertical as possible during the move.
  8. Lift under the metal enclosure on each side of the unit, keeping the instrument as close to vertical as possible during the move.
  9. Gently place the instrument in its new location.
  10. Level the instrument to prevent moist membranes from sliding on the scan surface and to keep the instrument from tilting.
  11. Level the instrument to prevent liquid from pooling in the imaging tray and to keep the instrument from tilting.
  12. Level the instrument to prevent the imaging drawer from scraping the lab bench or table in front of the instrument and to keep the instrument from tilting.
  13. The instrument can be leveled by turning the threaded metal inserts located in each of the 4 plastic feet (Figure 1‑1).

. Turn the metal inserts on the feet to adjust the height.
