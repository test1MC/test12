Z-Factor Analysis

Calculating the for an assay produces a statistically derived, dimensionless value that can be used to characterize the quality of an assay during assay optimization and validation. The indicates whether the assay has sufficient dynamic range and low enough data variability to generate meaningful data.

  1. Set up an analysis on the image using the controls in the and groups on the (see In-Cell Western Analysis).

  2. Choose a normalization channel in which a housekeeping protein or protein stain is detected. The values from the other channel are divided by the values from the normalization channel to account for variations in cell number between wells.

  5. Select the type of well to mark by clicking the name in the Well Legend.
