Reduce Sample Loading Variability and Correct for Inevitable Variation

Small sample-to-sample and lane-to-lane variations are inevitable in Western blotting. A good experiment is designed to prevent variation and adequately correct for the inevitable variation that will occur. The following two factors are fundamental to the design of a rock solid Western blot experiment.

  1. Minimize amount of correction needed Normalization must be designed into the experiment to correct for variability that cannot be eliminated.

  2. Use normalization to correct for unavoidable variation Use a normalization method with appropriate ILC that can correct for variation at all stages of the Western blotting process. Choice of ILC affects how accurately you can correct for variability.
