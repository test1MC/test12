Normalization Requirements

For a normalization strategy to be accurate, it must conform to the core principle of normalization. To ensure that your strategy is accurate, verify that your ILC meets these requirements:

  1. The ILC must be unaffected by experimental conditions.

  2. The ILC and target must be detected within the same linear range.

  3. The ILC must not interfere with target detection.

Choosing and validating an ILC that meets these requirements is fundamental to the design and accuracy of a quantitative Western blot.

The Normalization Handbook (licor.com/handbook) describes how to choose and validate an appropriate internal loading control.

This guide focuses on examples of how normalization can go wrong, and provides tips for avoiding such problems.
